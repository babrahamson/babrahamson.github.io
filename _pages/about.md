I am Britt Abrahamson, a Postdoctoral Fellow working with [Emily Zakem](https://ezakem.scripts.mit.edu/emilyzakem/) at Carnegie Science. We are based at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) in Pasadena, California.

I am interested in how single-cell activity governs global-scale biogeochemical cycling across spatio-temporal scales, especially ecosystems experiencing dynamic oxygen fluctuations. The overlapping functional potentials of microorganisms make it difficult to predict their activity from genomic data alone. My current work focuses on developing physiologically resolved microbial models that capture the metabolic versatility underpinning the biogeochemistry of Oxygen Minimum Zones (OMZs). 

My previous work investigated the physiology of nitrifying microorgnaisms and the biogeochemistry of wetland ecosystems.
